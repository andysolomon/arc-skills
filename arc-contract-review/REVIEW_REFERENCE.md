# Contract Review Reference

Load this after identifying the document type.

## Red flags quick scan

| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- |
| Liability cap < 6 months | Inadequate protection |
| Uncapped indemnification | Unlimited exposure |
| `As-is` with no warranty | No recourse for defects |
| Unilateral suspension without notice | Service can vanish |
| Unilateral amendment rights | Terms can change |
| No termination for convenience | Locked in |
| Perpetual obligations, tails, non-competes | Indefinite exposure |
| Offshore jurisdiction such as BVI/Cayman | Expensive to enforce |
| Pre-signed conflict waivers | Reduced recourse for conflicts |
| `Sole discretion` favoring counterparty | No objective standard |
| Class action waiver plus mandatory arbitration | Limited remedies |
| Asymmetric assignment rights | They can assign, you cannot |

## Document type checklists

### NDA

- One-way or mutual?
- Definition of confidential information too broad?
- Standard exclusions included?
- Term: 3–5 years typical for general information; trade secrets may be indefinite.
- Representatives defined and bound?
- Residuals clause present?
- Non-solicit, standstill, no-contact, or public-announcement restrictions?
- Return/destruction and certification obligations?
- Compelled disclosure notice and protective-order cooperation?
- Injunctive relief and bond waiver?

### SaaS / MSA

- Liability cap: 12+ months standard; <6 months red flag.
- Uptime SLA: 99.9% with credits is common.
- Suspension rights: immediate or with notice/cure?
- Data ownership and data export rights.
- Price increase cap and renewal notice.
- Termination rights: mutual for cause/convenience? cure period?
- Subprocessor notice or approval rights.
- Vendor insurance: E&O, cyber, general liability.
- Security, privacy, DPA, audit rights, and breach notice.

### Payment / merchant agreement

- Reserve/holdback amount, duration, release conditions.
- Chargeback/fraud liability and caps.
- Network rules incorporated by reference and available?
- Auto-debit authority and notice.
- Settlement timing.
- Volume commitments and shortfall penalties.
- Suspension rights and termination tail.
- Audit rights: frequency, notice, cost allocation.
- PCI compliance cost allocation.

### M&A agreement

- Purchase price mix: cash, stock, earnout.
- Earnout mechanics: measurement, discretion, audit rights, acceleration.
- Escrow/holdback: 10–15% for 12–18 months typical.
- Representation survival: 12–24 months general; longer for fundamental reps.
- Indemnification cap: 10–20% of purchase price typical.
- Basket: true deductible vs tipping.
- Sandbagging: pro-buyer or anti-sandbagging.
- Non-compete: 2–3 years, reasonable geography.
- Working capital target/collar/true-up.
- MAC definition and market-condition carve-outs.

### Finder / broker agreement

- Fee percentage and fee base.
- Whether employment comp, stock, earnouts, or assumed liabilities count in deal value.
- Covered buyer definition and prior-relationship carve-outs.
- Tail period: 12–18 months common; perpetual red flag.
- Exclusivity and termination rights.
- Minimum fee.
- Joint representation / conflict waiver.
- Escrow deduction or auto-pay from proceeds.
- Broker-dealer registration if securities are involved.

## Risk categories

- Document basics: name/type, parties, dates, expiration, renewal, status, blanks.
- Term and termination: duration, convenience, cause, post-termination services, survival, suspension, cure periods.
- Assignment and control: anti-assignment, change of control, consent, asymmetric rights.
- Financial: payment, price adjustments, MFN, minimum commitments, volume restrictions, audit, reserves, auto-debit.
- Liability and risk: limitation of liability, carve-outs, indemnity, insurance, warranty, as-is, exclusive remedy.
- IP and confidentiality: ownership, licenses, non-compete, non-solicit, exclusivity, non-disparagement, residuals, feedback.
- Dispute resolution: governing law, venue, arbitration, jury waiver, class waiver, offshore jurisdiction.
- Special provisions: ROFR/ROFO/ROFN, revenue share, joint IP, source escrow, perpetual license, data export, SLA, sublicensing, unilateral amendment.

## Market benchmarks

| Provision | Standard | Yellow Flag | Red Flag |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Liability cap | 12 months' fees | 6–11 months | <6 months |
| Non-compete duration | 1–2 years | 3–4 years | 5+ years |
| Non-compete geography | Where business operates | State-wide | Nationwide |
| Auto-renewal notice | 90+ days | 60–89 days | <60 days |
| Termination notice | Mutual, 60–90 days | One-sided, 30 days | Immediate |
| Indemnification | Mutual, capped | Asymmetric | Uncapped |
| Rep survival (M&A) | 12–18 months general | 24–30 months | 36+ months |
| Escrow (M&A) | 10–15% for 12–18 months | 15–20% for 18–24 months | >20% or >24 months |
| Confidentiality (NDA) | 3 years general | 2 years | 5+ years |
| Fee tail (broker) | 12–18 months | 24 months | Perpetual |
| SLA uptime | 99.9% with credits | 99.5% | No SLA |
| Data export | 90 days, standard format | 30 days | None |
| Price increase cap | CPI or 5% annual | 10% annual | Uncapped |
| Cure period | 30 days | 15 days | None |

## Negotiability guide

| Rating | Meaning | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| High | Usually accepted | Mutual termination, cure periods, data export |
| Medium | Depends on leverage | Liability cap increase, price caps |
| Low | Rarely changed | Network rules, regulatory requirements |
| None | Non-negotiable | Card-network mandates, banking regulations |

Power dynamic factors:

- Large customer + small vendor = more leverage.
- Startup + enterprise vendor = less leverage.
- Competitive market = more leverage.
- Sole-source vendor = less leverage.
- Regulated terms may be legally required.

## Jurisdiction notes

- Non-competes: California, North Dakota, Oklahoma, and Minnesota generally void many non-competes; other states use reasonableness tests.
- Delaware: corporate-friendly and predictable.
- New York: common for financial agreements.
- California: employee-friendly and common in tech.
- BVI/Cayman/offshore venues: can be expensive to litigate and may be a red flag.
- AAA/JAMS arbitration: standard US commercial forums.
- SIAC/LCIA: international and often expensive.
